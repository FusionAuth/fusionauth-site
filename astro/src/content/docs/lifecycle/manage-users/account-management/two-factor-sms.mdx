## Add a Two Factor Method - SMS

SMS two factor is not enabled by default. Configure your tenant by navigating to <strong>Tenants -> Edit Tenant -> Multi-Factor</strong>.

### Enable MFA method on Tenant (Admin Facing)

<img src="/img/docs/lifecycle/manage-users/account-management/enable-sms-tenant.png" alt="Configure Tenant SMS"  width="1200" />

You can enable the SMS factor on the tenant level by following these steps:

- Toggle this Two Factor method by clicking `enabled`
- Select a `messenger` (previously created, see [documentation](/docs/customize/email-and-messages/))
- Select a `template` (FusionAuth ships with a default to be customized if needed)

### Enable SMS Factor from Account Management (User Facing)

<img src="/img/docs/lifecycle/manage-users/account-management/account-management-index-crop.png" alt="Account Management Index"  width="1200"  role="bottom-cropped" />

1. Navigate back to your account page.
2. Click `Manage two-factor`
3. Click `Add two-factor`
4. There will be an option for SMS.

<img src="/img/docs/lifecycle/manage-users/account-management/add-factors-home-crop.png" alt="Add Factors"  width="1200"  role="bottom-cropped" />
<img src="/img/docs/lifecycle/manage-users/account-management/sms-configure-cropped.png" alt="Add SMS Authenticator"  width="1200" />

Next,

1. Enter your phone number.
2. Click on  `Send a one-time-code`.
3. Enter the `Verification Code` 
4. Click `Enable`.

### Recovery Codes (User Facing)

Now you will be presented with recovery codes. Save these in a safe space.

<img src="/img/docs/lifecycle/manage-users/account-management/mfa-recovery-codes-crop.png" alt="Recovery Codes"  width="1200"  role="bottom-cropped" />

<Icon name="trophy" /> Success!

Upon the next login, you will be prompted for a code sent by SMS code in addition to your password.

### See It in Action (User Facing)

With SMS MFA enabled, if you log out and log back in you will be presented with the following screen in addition to the typical login screen.
