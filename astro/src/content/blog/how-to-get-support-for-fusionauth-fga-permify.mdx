[FusionAuth FGA by Permify](/docs/extend/fine-grained-authorization/fusionauth-fga) is an open-source authorization service, now part of [FusionAuth](https://fusionauth.io/). Following FusionAuth's [acquisition of Permify](/blog/fusionauth-permify-pr), the product is now called FusionAuth FGA by Permify — combining Permify's relationship-based, Google Zanzibar-inspired authorization engine with FusionAuth's deploy-anywhere identity platform. It handles fine-grained access control (FGA), deciding what authenticated users are allowed to do within your application, with support for RBAC, ABAC, and ReBAC at scale.

If you're using FusionAuth FGA by Permify (or considering it), you'll eventually need help. This guide covers the two ways to get support: through the free community Discord or through a paid enterprise plan with FusionAuth.

## Community Support

The [Permify Discord server](https://discord.gg/permify) is the main free community channel where you can ask questions and get help from maintainers and other users. To join:

1. Go to [discord.gg/permify](https://discord.gg/permify).
2. Create a Discord account if you don't already have one, or log in.
3. Click "Accept Invite" to join the server.

![Discord join page for Permify](/img/blogs/permify-support/discord-join-invite.png)

When you first join, the server asks a couple of onboarding questions about how you're using FusionAuth FGA by Permify and what features you're interested in. You can answer these or skip them.

![Onboarding question about Permify usage](/img/blogs/permify-support/discord-onboarding-usage.png)

Once you're in, head to the `#permify-help-forum` channel to ask questions. (The older `#permify-help` channel is being deprecated in favor of the forum channel.) There are also channels for `#general` discussion, `#schema` help, and `#feedback`.

![Discord channel list and announcements](/img/blogs/permify-support/discord-announcements-channel.png)

Both community members and Permify maintainers respond to questions here, and many questions get a response within a couple of hours. However, more complex questions may take longer, and there's no guarantees.

### Strengths of Community Support

- Free to use, with no account or license required beyond Discord.
- Active community with responsive maintainers. Most questions get answered within hours.
- Good for getting started, exploring FusionAuth FGA by Permify's features, and troubleshooting common issues.

### Limitations of Community Support

- No guaranteed response times. Support is best-effort, and complex questions may take longer to get a reply.
- Not covered by any SLA. If your production system goes down at 2am, there is no obligation for anyone to respond.
- Answers come from the community as well as maintainers, so quality can vary.

## Paid Enterprise Support

If you're running FusionAuth FGA by Permify in production and need guaranteed support, FusionAuth offers Permify support as part of its [Enterprise plan](https://fusionauth.io/pricing). This is separate from the community channels and gives you direct access to FusionAuth's engineering team — including Permify experts who built the original open-source project and joined FusionAuth with the acquisition.

When you get an Enterprise support license, you can choose how to deploy FusionAuth FGA by Permify:

- **FusionAuth Cloud**: Run a fully managed, highly available FusionAuth FGA by Permify instance in FusionAuth Cloud, with no infrastructure to manage.
- **Self-hosted**: Deploy FusionAuth FGA by Permify in your own infrastructure, keeping full control over your data and environment.

### What You Get

- 24/7 support via email and phone.
- A private Slack channel with FusionAuth's team.
- Support tickets through the [FusionAuth account portal](https://fusionauth.io/docs/operate/troubleshooting/technical-support), with response time SLAs.
- Solution architect guidance for your implementation.

### How to Get It

Permify support is included with the FusionAuth Enterprise plan. To get started:

1. Visit the [FusionAuth pricing page](https://fusionauth.io/pricing) to review the Enterprise plan details.
2. [Contact FusionAuth's sales team](https://fusionauth.io/contact) to discuss your requirements. A sales representative and sales engineer can help answer specific questions about your use case and hosting preferences.
3. Once you have an Enterprise license, log in to your FusionAuth account portal. You'll see a support button in the lower right corner where you can open and track support tickets.

### Strengths of Paid Support

- SLA-backed response times, so you know when to expect a reply.
- Direct access to FusionAuth's engineering team rather than relying on community volunteers.
- 24/7 availability, including phone support for urgent, production impacting issues.
- Private Slack channel for ongoing communication with your support team.
- Includes a license for advanced features of FusionAuth; if you are in the market for a CIAM solution you can use that as well.
- Flexible deployment: FusionAuth-managed cloud hosting or your own infrastructure.

### Limitations of Paid Support

- Requires a FusionAuth Enterprise license; contact sales for pricing based on your requirements.
- Best suited for teams running FusionAuth FGA by Permify in production. If you're in early evaluation or running a small project, the cost may not be justified yet.

## Which Option Is Right for You?

For most people getting started with FusionAuth FGA by Permify, the community Discord server is the right place to begin. It's active, free, and covers the majority of common questions.

If you're running FusionAuth FGA by Permify in production where downtime has real consequences, the Enterprise plan gives you the SLA and direct engineering access that community support cannot. [Contact FusionAuth](https://fusionauth.io/contact) to discuss whether the Enterprise plan fits your needs.
